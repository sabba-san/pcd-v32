🔑 What is the Defect Liability Period (DLP)?

The DLP is essentially your property's "warranty period". Under the Malaysian Housing Development Act (HDA), for 24 months from the date you receive your keys (Vacant Possession or VP), the developer is legally required to fix any defects, shrinkages, or faults in your new home at their own cost.

⭐ Best Practices & Tips

  • Find your VP Date: Keep your "Notice of Vacant Possession" letter handy. The 24-month clock starts on the date printed on that letter!
  • Lighting is everything: When using the AI Image Scanner, ensure the room is brightly lit so the AI can see the cracks clearly.
  • Be specific: In the Notice Letter tool, write defects clearly (e.g., "Master Bedroom - Water stain on the ceiling corner" instead of just "Ceiling broken").
  • Keep copies: Always keep a photocopy of any signed Notice Letter you give to the developer.
